During an Earthquake

  • Drop to the ground, take cover under sturdy furniture, and hold on
  • Stay away from windows, outside doors, and walls
  • If outdoors, stay in the open away from buildings, streetlights, and utility wires
  • If in a vehicle, pull over to a clear location and stop
  • Wait until the shaking stops before moving

After an Earthquake

  • Check yourself and others for injuries
  • Inspect your home for damage, especially gas leaks
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Listen to local news for emergency information
  • Stay away from damaged areas unless authorities request assistance

During a Flood

  • Listen to local alerts and warning systems
  • If told to evacuate, do so immediately
  • Never walk, swim, or drive through flood waters
  • Stay off bridges over fast-moving water
  • Move to higher ground or a higher floor

After a Flood

  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe
  • Avoid walking or driving through flood waters
  • Use caution when entering buildings
  • Wear protective clothing during cleanup
  • Be aware of electrocution risk if electrical systems are damaged

Before a Cyclone

During a Cyclone

  • Stay indoors and away from windows
  • Take shelter in the strongest part of your house
  • Listen to a battery-powered radio for updates
  • Turn off utilities if instructed to do so
  • If evacuation is ordered, leave immediately

After a Cyclone

  • Stay away from damaged areas
  • Watch out for fallen power lines
  • Avoid floodwaters which may be contaminated
  • Check on neighbors, especially the elderly
  • Document damage for insurance purposes
