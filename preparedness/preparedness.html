Disaster Preparedness Categories

Earthquake

Prepare for seismic events with these guidelines.

Earthquake Preparedness

Before an earthquake:

  • Secure heavy furniture to walls
  • Know the safe spots in each room
  • Have emergency supplies ready

During an earthquake:

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Stay away from windows
  • If outdoors, move to an open area

Flood

Essential preparedness for flood situations.

Flood Preparedness

Before a flood:

  • Prepare an emergency kit
  • Know evacuation routes
  • Move valuable items to higher levels

During a flood:

  • Avoid walking through flowing water
  • Do not drive through flooded areas
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts

Cyclone

Be ready for high winds and heavy rainfall.

Cyclone Preparedness

Before a cyclone:

  • Secure loose items outside your home
  • Prepare emergency kit and evacuation plan
  • Board up windows and secure doors

During a cyclone:

  • Stay indoors away from windows
  • Listen to emergency broadcasts
  • If evacuating, leave early

Landslide

Safety measures for areas prone to landslides.

Landslide Preparedness

Before a landslide:

  • Be aware of warning signs like tilting trees
  • Prepare an emergency kit
  • Know evacuation routes

During a landslide:

  • Evacuate immediately if possible
  • Move to higher ground
  • Watch for flooding that may accompany landslides

Do's and Don'ts During Disasters

Do's

  • Drop to the ground
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture
  • Hold on until shaking stops
  • Stay indoors until the shaking stops
  • Be prepared for aftershocks

Don'ts

  • Don't run outside during shaking
  • Don't use elevators
  • Don't stand in doorways
  • Don't light matches or candles
  • Don't move severely injured people

Do's

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Listen to official instructions
  • Turn off utilities if instructed
  • Prepare an emergency kit
  • Avoid contaminated water

Don'ts

  • Don't walk through flowing water
  • Don't drive through flooded areas
  • Don't touch electrical equipment if wet
  • Don't use appliances that have been wet
  • Don't ignore evacuation orders

Do's

  • Secure your home
  • Stock up on emergency supplies
  • Stay updated with weather reports
  • Keep important documents in waterproof containers
  • Follow evacuation orders immediately

Don'ts

  • Don't go outside during the cyclone
  • Don't stay in mobile homes
  • Don't ignore warnings
  • Don't use candles for lighting
  • Don't venture out until all-clear is given

Do's

  • Listen for unusual sounds
  • Evacuate if instructed
  • Watch for changes in water flow
  • Move away from the landslide area quickly
  • Contact local authorities about hazards

Don'ts

  • Don't stay in the path of a landslide
  • Don't return to affected area until authorities allow
  • Don't build near steep slopes
  • Don't ignore warning signs
  • Don't remove debris without professional help

Do's

  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Follow evacuation orders from local authorities
  • Stay away from beaches and coastal areas
  • Listen to emergency alerts and warnings
  • Help others evacuate if possible

Don'ts

  • Don't ignore tsunami warnings
  • Don't stay near coastal waters to watch the waves
  • Don't return to low-lying areas until officials say it's safe
  • Don't drive through flooded roads
  • Don't rely solely on visual cues to detect a tsunami
